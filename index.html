Overview

Teleoperation systems are evolving beyond traditional interfaces by integrating sensorimotor augmentation to align with human proprioceptive and cognitive capabilities. While advancements in robotics and AI have improved remote operations, challenges persist in reducing cognitive load, enhancing situational awareness, and ensuring intuitive human-robot collaboration. This workshop introduces a paradigm shift toward human-interaction-oriented (HIO) frameworks, integrating multimodal sensory feedback (such as haptic and proprioceptive systems) with adaptive motor control strategies. This approach aims to bridge the gap between human intent and robotic execution, enhancing synergy in interactive applications.

Figure 1: Human-interaction-oriented (HIO) teleoperation: Modalities, subjects, and criteria across diverse applications.

Aim and Scope

Unlike previous workshops focused on unilateral teleoperation, this event uniquely explores sensorimotor integration across diverse applications - surgery, marine exploration, and nuclear decommissioning - where human adaptability and real-time feedback are critical. Building upon the success of the 4th edition at IROS 2025 in Hangzhou, this 5th edition introduces new themes in an afternoon half-day format. Topics include:

  • Multimodal sensory fusion for enriched operator perception.
  • Motor control augmentation via AI-driven exoskeletons and VR/AR interfaces.
  • Human adaptability to dynamic teleoperation environments.
  • Foundation-model-driven teleoperation control. [NEW]
  • Digital twin-enhanced operator training. [NEW]
  • Adaptive shared autonomy in cluttered environments. [NEW]

The half-day workshop features confirmed speakers from academia, industry and end users, an innovative Mentor-Mentee Network (MMN) fostering cross-disciplinary collaboration, interactive demos of cutting-edge devices (e.g., haptic gloves, vision-based tactile sensors), and a hands-on Teleoperation Challenge Session. By integrating robotics, neuroscience, and human-computer interaction, this workshop seeks to reconceptualize teleoperation through human-centered sensorimotor enhancements, prioritizing safety and intuitive interaction to enable next-generation collaborative frameworks.

Call for Contributions

We invite submissions in the following categories:

  • Research Paper (up to 6 pages + references) - Original research in HIO teleoperation, sensorimotor augmentation, and related fields.
  • Work-in-Progress (up to 2 pages + references) - Preliminary results, emerging ideas, or exploratory studies.
  • Demo Abstract (1 page) - Demonstrations of teleoperation systems, haptic interfaces, or related technologies.

All papers must be submitted in PDF format following the IROS double-column format. Templates are available at the IEEE templates page. Accepted papers will be presented as posters. Selected papers featured in the Poster Spotlight initiative.

Submission: Please submit via email to z.wang82@lancaster.ac.uk with the subject line IROS 2026 WS Submission.

Teleoperation Challenge

New for the 5th Edition

We are introducing a Hands-on Teleoperation Challenge where small teams (3 to 4 participants) collaboratively perform teleoperated manipulation tasks using haptic devices and tactile sensor systems. Teams compete on task completion time, precision, and operator cognitive load. No prior registration required - teams formed on-site.

Industry Challenge: Remote Industrial Robot Deployment (in partnership with SANY Group)

SANY Group — a global leader in heavy machinery and construction equipment — will present a set of open deployment challenges drawn directly from their real-world teleoperation field experience. Participants are invited to propose solutions, approaches, or research directions to address these problems.

Challenge topics include:

  • High-latency teleoperation of construction robots in unstructured outdoor environments
  • Operator situational awareness under degraded or limited sensory feedback
  • Human-machine collaborative control strategies for heavy-duty manipulation
  • Robustness, fault tolerance, and safety in large-scale remote robot deployment

This is not an on-site competition — SANY engineers will introduce each challenge and engage with participants throughout the workshop to explore viable research and engineering solutions together.
